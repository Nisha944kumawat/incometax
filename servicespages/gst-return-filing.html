GST Return Filing




Introduction

GST Return Filing is the process of reporting details of sales, purchases, tax collected, and tax paid to the government. Every registered taxpayer must file GST returns periodically to ensure compliance with GST laws. Timely and accurate return filing helps avoid penalties and ensures smooth business operations.

What are the Benefits of GST Return Filing?

  • Ensures legal compliance with GST regulations
  • Helps avoid late fees and penalties
  • Enables smooth Input Tax Credit (ITC) claims
  • Maintains proper financial records
  • Enhances business credibility
  • Prevents notices and legal complications

What are the Eligibility Criteria for GST Return Filing?

  • All registered GST taxpayers must file returns
  • Regular taxpayers filing GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B
  • Composition scheme taxpayers filing CMP-08 & GSTR-4
  • E-commerce operators and TDS/TCS deductors
  • Businesses even with NIL transactions must file returns

What are the Documents Required for GST Return Filing?

  • Sales invoices (outward supplies)
  • Purchase invoices (inward supplies)
  • Debit/Credit notes
  • GSTIN details
  • Bank statements (if required)
  • Previous return filings
  • E-way bill data (if applicable)

What are the GST Late Fee & Penalty Details?

  • ₹50 per day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST) for delay in filing returns
  • ₹20 per day for NIL returns
  • Interest @18% per annum on outstanding tax liability
  • Continuous default may lead to suspension of GSTIN

Due Dates of Filing

  • GSTR-1 (Monthly): 11th of next month
  • GSTR-3B: 20th of next month
  • QRMP Scheme: Quarterly return filing (13th of month after quarter)
  • CMP-08 (Composition): 18th of month following quarter
  • GSTR-4 (Annual Composition): 30th April of next financial year
  • GSTR-9 (Annual Return): 31st December of next financial year

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is GST return filing mandatory?

Yes, all registered taxpayers must file returns, even if there is no business activity (NIL return).

2. What happens if I don’t file GST returns?

Late fees, interest, and possible suspension or cancellation of GST registration.

3. Can I revise a GST return after filing?

GST returns cannot be revised, but errors can be corrected in subsequent returns.

4. What is NIL return?

A NIL return is filed when there are no transactions during the period.

5. How often do I need to file GST returns?

Depends on your scheme—monthly, quarterly, or annually.

6. What is Input Tax Credit (ITC)?

ITC allows you to claim credit for GST paid on purchases and reduce your tax liability.
