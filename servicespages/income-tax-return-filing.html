Income Tax Return (ITR) Filing in India


Introduction

Income Tax Return (ITR) filing is the process of reporting your income, expenses, tax deductions, and taxes paid to the Income Tax Department of India. Filing your ITR on time ensures compliance with tax laws and helps avoid penalties. Whether you are a salaried individual, business owner, or freelancer, timely ITR filing is essential for financial credibility and smooth financial planning.

What are the Benefits of ITR Filing?

  • Legal compliance with Income Tax laws
  • Easy loan approval (home loan, car loan, etc.)
  • Helps in visa processing
  • Claim tax refunds easily
  • Carry forward losses (business/capital losses)
  • Proof of income for financial transactions
  • Avoid penalties and notices

What are the Eligibility Criteria for ITR Filing?

You must file ITR if:

  • Your total income exceeds the basic exemption limit
  • You want to claim a tax refund
  • You have foreign income or assets
  • You are a company or firm (mandatory filing)
  • You have deposited high amounts in bank accounts
  • You want to carry forward losses
  • You fall under specific conditions as per Income Tax Act

What are the Documents Required for ITR Filing?

  • PAN Card
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Form 16 (for salaried individuals)
  • Bank statements
  • Investment proofs (80C, 80D, etc.)
  • TDS certificates (Form 16A, 26Q, etc.)
  • Capital gains statements (if applicable)
  • Business financial statements (for business owners)
  • Previous year ITR (if available)

What are the Late Fee & Penalty Details?

  • ₹1,000: If income is below ₹5 lakh
  • ₹5,000: If income exceeds ₹5 lakh (filed after due date)
  • Interest under Section 234A, 234B, 234C may apply
  • Losses cannot be carried forward if filed late

Due Dates of Filing ITR

  • Individuals (Non-audit cases): 31st July
  • Businesses (Audit cases): 31st October
  • Revised/Belated Return: 31st December
  • (Dates may change as per government notification)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is ITR filing mandatory for salaried individuals?

Yes, if your income exceeds the exemption limit or you meet certain conditions.

2. Can I file ITR after the due date?

Yes, but with a late fee and some restrictions.

3. How long does it take to get a refund?

Usually 7–45 days after verification.

4. What happens if I don’t file ITR?

You may face penalties, interest, and legal consequences.

5. Which ITR form should I use?

Depends on your income type (ITR-1, ITR-2, ITR-3, etc.).
