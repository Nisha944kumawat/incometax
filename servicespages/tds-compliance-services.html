TDS & Compliance Services




Introduction

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) is a crucial compliance requirement under the Income Tax Act, where tax is deducted at the time of making specified payments such as salary, rent, professional fees, and contractor payments. Businesses and professionals are required to deduct, deposit, and file TDS returns accurately and on time. At The Tax Titans, we provide end-to-end TDS compliance services to ensure smooth operations and full legal compliance.

What are the Benefits of TDS & Compliance Services?

  • Ensures compliance with Income Tax regulations
  • Avoids penalties and interest charges
  • Accurate deduction and timely deposit of TDS
  • Proper filing of TDS returns
  • Smooth issuance of TDS certificates (Form 16/16A)
  • Reduces risk of notices and scrutiny

Who is Required to Deduct TDS?

  • Businesses and companies
  • Partnership firms & LLPs
  • Professionals (CA, Doctors, Consultants, etc.)
  • Employers deducting TDS on salaries
  • Individuals liable under specific sections

What are the Key TDS Compliance Requirements?

  • Deduction of TDS at applicable rates
  • Timely deposit of TDS to the government
  • Filing of quarterly TDS returns (24Q, 26Q, 27Q)
  • Issuance of TDS certificates
  • Reconciliation with Form 26AS

What Documents are Required?

  • PAN of deductor & deductee
  • TAN (Tax Deduction Account Number)
  • Payment details (salary, contractor, rent, etc.)
  • Bank challans (TDS payment proof)
  • Previous TDS returns (if applicable)
  • Books of accounts

What are the TDS Late Fee & Penalty Details?

  • ₹200 per day under Section 234E (for late filing)
  • Penalty under Section 271H (₹10,000 to ₹1,00,000)
  • Interest @1% or 1.5% per month for delay in deduction/deposit
  • Disallowance of expenses in income tax (in some cases)

Due Dates of TDS Compliance

TDS Payment Due Dates:

  • 7th of next month (for most cases)
  • 30th April (for March deductions)

TDS Return Filing Due Dates:

  • Q1 (Apr–Jun): 31st July
  • Q2 (Jul–Sep): 31st October
  • Q3 (Oct–Dec): 31st January
  • Q4 (Jan–Mar): 31st May

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is TDS?

TDS is tax deducted at source while making certain payments.

2. Is TAN mandatory for TDS?

Yes, TAN is required for deducting and filing TDS returns.

3. What happens if TDS is not deducted?

You may face penalties, interest, and disallowance of expenses.

4. Can TDS returns be revised?

Yes, TDS returns can be corrected or revised.

5. Who receives TDS credit?

The person whose income is deducted gets the credit.
