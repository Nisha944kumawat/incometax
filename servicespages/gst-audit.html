GST Audit


Introduction

GST Audit refers to the examination of records, returns, and other documents maintained by a registered taxpayer under the GST law to verify the correctness of turnover, taxes paid, refunds claimed, and input tax credit (ITC) availed.

Although the mandatory GST audit by a Chartered Accountant has been removed, GST authorities can still conduct audits, and businesses must ensure proper compliance, reconciliation, and accurate reporting to avoid penalties and notices.

Benefits of GST Audit

  • Ensures Accurate GST Compliance
  • Identifies Errors in Returns & Filings
  • Helps in Proper ITC Claim and Utilization
  • Reduces Risk of Notices & Penalties
  • Improves Financial Transparency
  • Strengthens Record Keeping
  • Builds Confidence during Departmental Audit

Eligibility / Applicability of GST Audit

  • Businesses selected by GST authorities for audit
  • Taxpayers with discrepancies in GST returns
  • Businesses with high turnover or complex transactions
  • Entities flagged for mismatch in GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, and books
  • Voluntary GST audit for internal compliance and risk management

Documents Required for GST Audit

  • GST Returns (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, GSTR-9 if applicable)
  • Books of Accounts and Financial Statements
  • Sales and Purchase Registers
  • Input Tax Credit (ITC) Records
  • E-way Bills and Invoice Copies
  • Bank Statements and Reconciliation
  • Debit/Credit Notes
  • Previous GST Audit Reports (if any)

GST Audit Penalty & Compliance Details

  • No specific late fee for GST audit itself
  • Penalty for incorrect reporting or non-compliance: ₹10,000 or 10% of tax due (whichever is higher)
  • Fraud cases: 100% of tax amount
  • Interest @18% per annum on unpaid tax
  • Penalties may arise during departmental audit findings

Due Dates / Timeline

  • No fixed due date for GST audit
  • Departmental audit notice: Minimum 15 days prior notice
  • Audit completion: Within 3 months (extendable to 6 months)
  • Annual Return (GSTR-9, if applicable): 31st December of next financial year

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. Is GST audit mandatory now?

No, mandatory audit by CA has been removed, but departmental audits still apply.

Q2. Who conducts GST audit?

GST officers or department authorities.

Q3. Can I do GST audit voluntarily?

Yes, businesses can conduct internal GST review for compliance.

Q4. What happens if discrepancies are found?

Tax demand, interest, and penalties may be imposed.

Q5. What is the purpose of GST audit?

To verify correctness of GST returns and compliance.

Q6. Is GSTR-9 mandatory?

Applicable based on turnover and government notifications.

Q7. How to avoid GST audit issues?

Maintain proper records, reconcile returns regularly, and file accurate data.
