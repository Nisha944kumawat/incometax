Final Return (GSTR-10)



Final Return (GSTR-10) Filing in India


Introduction

GSTR-10, also known as the Final Return, is required to be filed by taxpayers whose GST registration has been cancelled or surrendered. This return ensures that all outstanding tax liabilities are settled and provides a complete summary of closing stock and liabilities. Filing GSTR-10 is essential to formally close GST compliance and avoid future legal complications.

What are the Benefits of Filing GSTR-10?

  • Ensures proper closure of GST registration
  • Avoids future notices and penalties
  • Clears all pending tax liabilities
  • Maintains compliance with GST laws
  • Provides transparency in final tax settlement
  • Protects business from legal consequences

What are the Eligibility Criteria for Filing GSTR-10?

  • Taxpayers whose GST registration is cancelled or surrendered
  • Businesses discontinuing operations
  • Entities merging, transferring, or restructuring operations
  • Not applicable to composition taxpayers or non-resident taxable persons

What are the Documents Required for GSTR-10 Filing?

  • GSTIN and cancellation order
  • Details of closing stock (inputs, semi-finished, finished goods)
  • Input Tax Credit (ITC) details on stock
  • Tax payable details
  • Previous GST returns
  • Financial statements (if required)

What are the GST Late Fee & Penalty Details?

  • Late fee of ₹200 per day (₹100 CGST + ₹100 SGST)
  • Maximum late fee capped at ₹10,000
  • Interest @18% per annum on unpaid tax
  • Non-filing may lead to legal action and recovery proceedings

Due Date of Filing

  • GSTR-10 must be filed within 3 months from the date of cancellation or cancellation order, whichever is later

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is GSTR-10?

GSTR-10 is the final return filed when GST registration is cancelled.

2. Is filing GSTR-10 mandatory?

Yes, for all taxpayers whose registration is cancelled (except certain categories like composition taxpayers).

3. What happens if GSTR-10 is not filed?

Late fees, penalties, and legal action may be initiated by the GST department.

4. Can I revise GSTR-10 after filing?

No, once filed, it cannot be revised.

5. What details are required in GSTR-10?

Details of closing stock, tax liability, and ITC on stock must be reported.

6. How long does it take to file GSTR-10?

With proper documentation, it can be filed quickly within a few days.
