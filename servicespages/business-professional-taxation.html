Introduction

Business & Professional Taxation focuses on managing income tax compliance, filings, and advisory for businesses, firms, and self-employed professionals. It includes maintaining proper financial records, calculating tax liabilities, and filing accurate Income Tax Returns (ITR). At The Tax Titans, we help businesses and professionals stay compliant while optimizing their tax position.

What are the Benefits of ITR Filing?

  • Ensures legal compliance with Income Tax laws
  • Helps in loan and credit approvals
  • Enables claiming of tax refunds
  • Facilitates carry forward of business losses
  • Acts as proof of income
  • Avoids penalties and notices
  • Builds financial credibility

What are the Eligibility Criteria for ITR Filing?

You must file ITR if:

  • Your income exceeds the basic exemption limit
  • You are running a business or profession
  • You want to claim a tax refund
  • You have foreign income or assets
  • You want to carry forward losses
  • You are required under specific conditions of the Income Tax Act

What are the Documents Required for ITR Filing?

  • PAN Card
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Bank statements
  • Profit & Loss Account / Balance Sheet (for business)
  • Investment proofs
  • TDS certificates (Form 16/16A)
  • GST details (if applicable)
  • Previous year ITR

What are the ITR Late Fee & Penalty Details?

  • ₹1,000: If income up to ₹5 lakh
  • ₹5,000: If income exceeds ₹5 lakh
  • Interest under Sections 234A, 234B, 234C
  • Losses cannot be carried forward if filed late
  • Possible notices for non-compliance

Due Dates of Filing ITR

  • Individuals & Professionals (Non-audit): 31st July
  • Businesses requiring audit: 31st October
  • Belated/Revised Return: 31st December
  • (Dates may change as per government updates)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is ITR filing mandatory for professionals?

Yes, if income exceeds the exemption limit or if required under law.

2. Which ITR form is applicable for business/profession?

ITR-3 or ITR-4 depending on income type and scheme.

3. Can I file ITR after the due date?

Yes, but with late fees and certain restrictions.

4. What happens if I don’t file ITR?

You may face penalties, interest, and legal consequences.

5. Can I revise my ITR after filing?

Yes, before the prescribed deadline.
