Introduction

Goods and Services Tax (GST) Registration is the process through which a business becomes legally recognized under the GST law. Once registered, a business receives a unique GST Identification Number (GSTIN) and is authorized to collect GST from customers and claim input tax credit. GST registration is essential for ensuring compliance and smooth business operations across India.

What are the Benefits of GST Registration?

  • Legal authorization to collect GST from customers
  • Avail Input Tax Credit (ITC) on purchases
  • Seamless interstate trade without restrictions
  • Enhanced business credibility and trust
  • Eligibility to sell on e-commerce platforms
  • Better compliance and structured taxation

What are the Eligibility Criteria to Register for GST?

  • Businesses with turnover exceeding ₹40 lakh (₹20 lakh for service providers)
  • Interstate suppliers of goods or services
  • E-commerce sellers and operators
  • Casual taxable persons
  • Non-resident taxable persons
  • Businesses required to pay tax under reverse charge

What are the Documents Required for GST Registration?

  • PAN Card of the business or applicant
  • Aadhaar Card of promoters/partners/directors
  • Business registration proof (Partnership Deed, COI, etc.)
  • Address proof of principal place of business
  • Bank account details (cancelled cheque/bank statement)
  • Photographs of promoters
  • Digital Signature (for companies/LLPs)

What are the GST Late Fee & Penalty Details?

  • Late fee of ₹50 per day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST) for delay in filing returns
  • ₹20 per day for NIL returns
  • Interest @18% per annum on delayed tax payment
  • Penalties may increase based on non-compliance severity

Due Dates of Filing

  • GSTR-1 (Sales Return): 11th of the next month
  • GSTR-3B (Summary Return): 20th of the next month
  • Quarterly Returns (QRMP Scheme): Quarterly filing with monthly payment
  • Annual Return (GSTR-9): 31st December of next financial year

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is GST registration mandatory?

Yes, if your turnover exceeds the prescribed limit or if you fall under specific categories like interstate supply or e-commerce.

2. How long does GST registration take?

Usually 3–7 working days after successful document verification.

3. Can I apply for GST voluntarily?

Yes, even if your turnover is below the threshold, voluntary registration is allowed.

4. What happens if I don’t register under GST?

You may face penalties, legal consequences, and restrictions on business operations.

5. Can GST registration be cancelled?

Yes, it can be cancelled voluntarily or by the department due to non-compliance.

6. What is Input Tax Credit (ITC)?

ITC allows you to reduce your tax liability by claiming credit on GST paid on purchases.
