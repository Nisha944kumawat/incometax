FEMA & RBI Compliance


Introduction

FEMA & RBI Compliance involves adherence to regulations governing foreign exchange transactions, cross-border investments, and international financial dealings under the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA) and guidelines issued by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). These compliances are essential for businesses and individuals dealing with foreign transactions. At The Tax Titans, we provide expert assistance to ensure accurate reporting and seamless compliance with FEMA and RBI regulations.

What are the Benefits of FEMA & RBI Compliance?

  • Ensures compliance with foreign exchange laws
  • Avoids heavy penalties and legal complications
  • Smooth handling of international transactions
  • Proper reporting of foreign investments
  • Enhances credibility with global partners
  • Reduces risk of regulatory scrutiny

Who Needs These Services?

  • Companies receiving foreign investment (FDI)
  • Businesses making overseas payments
  • Importers and exporters
  • Startups with international investors
  • NRIs and foreign nationals
  • Companies with overseas subsidiaries

What are the Key Compliance Areas Covered?

  • FDI reporting (FC-GPR, FC-TRS)
  • External Commercial Borrowings (ECB) compliance
  • Overseas Direct Investment (ODI) reporting
  • Form 15CA & 15CB compliance
  • Annual Return on Foreign Liabilities and Assets (FLA)
  • Liaison/branch office compliance

What Documents are Required?

  • PAN of company/individual
  • Incorporation documents
  • Details of foreign investment or remittance
  • Agreements and contracts
  • Bank remittance documents
  • Shareholding structure
  • RBI approvals (if applicable)

What are the Penalties & Risks of Non-Compliance?

  • Heavy penalties under FEMA
  • Compounding proceedings
  • Restrictions on foreign transactions
  • Legal notices and regulatory action
  • Impact on business reputation

Due Dates of Compliance

  • FLA Return: 15th July annually
  • FC-GPR: Within 30 days of share allotment
  • FC-TRS: Within 60 days of transfer
  • Form 15CA/CB: Before remittance
  • (Due dates may vary as per RBI guidelines)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is FEMA compliance?

It refers to compliance with foreign exchange laws in India.

2. Is RBI reporting mandatory for foreign investment?

Yes, timely reporting is compulsory.

3. What is FDI reporting?

It is reporting foreign investment in Indian companies.

4. What happens if FEMA compliance is not done?

Heavy penalties and legal consequences may arise.

5. Who regulates FEMA compliance?

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI).
