GST Audit & Reconciliation




Introduction

GST Audit & Reconciliation is a critical process that ensures the accuracy of GST returns, financial records, and tax compliance. It involves verifying the correctness of data reported in GST returns with books of accounts and identifying discrepancies. Proper reconciliation helps businesses avoid notices, penalties, and ensures smooth compliance with GST regulations.

What are the Benefits of GST Audit & Reconciliation?

  • Ensures accuracy in GST filings and financial records
  • Identifies mismatches between returns and books
  • Helps in proper utilization of Input Tax Credit (ITC)
  • Reduces risk of GST notices and penalties
  • Improves financial transparency and control
  • Strengthens compliance and audit readiness

What are the Eligibility Criteria for GST Audit & Reconciliation?

  • GST-registered businesses
  • Businesses with high transaction volumes
  • Companies facing discrepancies in GST returns
  • Businesses undergoing GST audits or scrutiny
  • Organizations aiming for accurate financial reporting
  • Businesses seeking compliance and risk management

What are the Documents Required for GST Audit & Reconciliation?

  • GST returns (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, GSTR-9, etc.)
  • Sales and purchase registers
  • Input Tax Credit (ITC) records
  • Financial statements and books of accounts
  • E-way bills and e-invoices (if applicable)
  • Bank statements
  • Previous audit reports (if any)

What are the GST Late Fee & Penalty Details?

  • ₹50 per day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST) for delayed return filing
  • ₹20 per day for NIL returns
  • Interest @18% per annum on delayed tax payments
  • Penalties for mismatches, incorrect filings, or non-compliance
  • Risk of GST registration suspension in severe cases

Due Dates of Filing

  • GSTR-1: 11th of the next month
  • GSTR-3B: 20th of the next month
  • Annual Return (GSTR-9): 31st December of next financial year
  • Reconciliation Statement (if applicable): Along with annual return
  • Other filings as per business category

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is GST Audit & Reconciliation?

It is the process of verifying GST returns with financial records to ensure accuracy and compliance.

2. Why is GST reconciliation important?

It helps identify errors, avoid penalties, and ensures correct tax reporting.

3. What happens if mismatches are found?

They must be corrected in subsequent returns to avoid penalties or notices.

4. Is GST audit mandatory?

GST audit requirements depend on turnover and applicable regulations.

5. How often should reconciliation be done?

Ideally monthly or quarterly for better accuracy and compliance.

6. Can reconciliation help in saving tax?

Yes, proper reconciliation ensures maximum ITC claim and avoids excess tax payment.
