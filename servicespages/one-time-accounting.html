One-Time Accounting




Introduction

One-Time Accounting is a professional accounting service designed for businesses that need accounting support for a specific period, project, pending work, or financial cleanup. It helps organize books, update records, reconcile accounts, and prepare financial data without requiring regular monthly accounting service.

Benefits of One-Time Accounting

  • Helps clear pending accounting work quickly
  • Cost-effective solution for specific accounting needs
  • Organizes scattered financial records
  • Supports tax filing, GST filing, and audit preparation
  • Corrects errors in previous accounting entries
  • Provides updated financial reports for decision-making
  • Useful for business owners who need temporary accounting support

Who Should Choose One-Time Accounting?

  • Businesses with pending or incomplete accounting records
  • Startups, proprietorship firms, partnerships, LLPs, and companies
  • Businesses preparing for GST, income tax, or audit filing
  • Business owners who need accounts cleanup for a specific period
  • Freelancers, consultants, traders, and service providers
  • Businesses requiring temporary support without long-term commitment

Documents Required for Accounting

  • Sales invoices and purchase bills
  • Bank statements
  • Expense bills and payment receipts
  • GST returns and GST registration details, if applicable
  • Previous accounting records
  • Cash transaction details
  • Loan statements, if any
  • Payroll and salary details, if applicable
  • Business PAN and basic business details

Services Covered Under One-Time Accounting

  • Backlog accounting and record updating
  • Sales and purchase entry posting
  • Bank reconciliation
  • Expense entry and categorization
  • Ledger review and correction
  • GST data preparation support
  • Accounts payable and receivable review
  • Financial statement preparation support

Penalty & Risk Aspects

  • Incomplete accounting may lead to tax filing errors
  • Incorrect financial records can result in notices or penalties
  • Missing invoices may create GST mismatch issues
  • Delayed accounting may affect audit and loan documentation
  • One-time accounting helps reduce compliance and financial reporting risks

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. What is One-Time Accounting?

One-Time Accounting is accounting support provided for a specific task, period, or pending accounting work.

Q2. Who needs One-Time Accounting?

Businesses with pending records, tax filing needs, audit preparation, or temporary accounting requirements can choose this service.

Q3. Can old accounting records be updated?

Yes, previous records, invoices, expenses, and bank entries can be updated and organized.

Q4. Is One-Time Accounting useful before GST filing?

Yes, it helps prepare accurate sales, purchase, and tax data for GST filing.

Q5. Can financial reports be prepared?

Yes, financial summaries and reports can be prepared based on available records.

Q6. Is there any long-term commitment?

No, this service is suitable for one-time or temporary accounting requirements.
