Stock & Inventory Audit


Introduction

Stock & Inventory Audit is the process of verifying the quantity, condition, and valuation of inventory held by a business. It ensures that the physical stock matches the records maintained in books and helps in identifying discrepancies, pilferage, wastage, or obsolete inventory.

This audit plays a crucial role in businesses dealing with goods, manufacturing, retail, and trading, ensuring accurate financial reporting and efficient inventory management.

Benefits of Stock & Inventory Audit

  • Ensures Accuracy of Stock Records
  • Detects Theft, Pilferage & Leakage
  • Improves Inventory Control Systems
  • Identifies Slow-moving or Obsolete Stock
  • Helps in Correct Stock Valuation
  • Enhances Operational Efficiency
  • Supports Better Decision Making & Cost Control

Eligibility / Applicability of Inventory Audit

  • Businesses with large inventory or multiple warehouses
  • Manufacturing, trading, and retail companies
  • Companies required by banks (for stock statements & working capital limits)
  • Entities seeking internal control and fraud prevention
  • Voluntary audits for better inventory management

Documents Required for Inventory Audit

  • Stock Register / Inventory Records
  • Purchase & Sales Invoices
  • Goods Receipt Notes (GRN) & Delivery Challans
  • Warehouse / Godown Records
  • Physical Stock Count Sheets
  • Bank Stock Statements (if submitted to banks)
  • Financial Statements
  • Previous Audit Reports (if any)

Audit Penalty & Risk Aspect

  • No direct statutory penalty for inventory audit itself
  • However, risks include financial misstatements due to incorrect stock valuation
  • Bank loan issues if stock statements are inaccurate
  • Loss due to theft, damage, or mismanagement
  • Tax/GST discrepancies arising from incorrect stock records

Frequency / Timeline

  • No fixed statutory due dates
  • Generally conducted monthly, quarterly, or annually
  • Year-end for financial reporting
  • As required by banks or management

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. Is inventory audit mandatory?

No, but it may be required by banks or for internal control purposes.

Q2. Who conducts stock audit?

Chartered Accountants, internal auditors, or professional firms.

Q3. Why is physical verification important?

It ensures actual stock matches recorded stock and detects discrepancies.

Q4. What is stock valuation?

Valuing inventory based on methods like FIFO, LIFO, or weighted average.

Q5. Can small businesses do inventory audit?

Yes, it helps reduce losses and improve efficiency.

Q6. How often should stock audit be done?

Depends on business size—monthly, quarterly, or annually.

Q7. What happens if discrepancies are found?

They must be investigated and corrected to avoid financial and operational risks.
