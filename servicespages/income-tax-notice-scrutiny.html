Introduction

Income Tax Notices & Scrutiny are issued by the Income Tax Department when discrepancies, high-value transactions, or mismatches are identified in your Income Tax Return (ITR). Receiving a notice can be stressful, but with the right guidance, it can be handled smoothly. At The Tax Titans, we provide expert assistance in responding to notices, handling scrutiny cases, and ensuring proper compliance with tax laws.

What are the Benefits of Professional Assistance in Notice Handling?

  • Accurate and timely response to notices
  • Reduced risk of penalties and legal consequences
  • Proper representation before tax authorities
  • Identification and correction of discrepancies
  • Peace of mind with expert support
  • Increased chances of favorable resolution

What are the Common Reasons for Receiving an Income Tax Notice?

  • Mismatch in income, TDS, or AIS data
  • Non-filing or delayed filing of ITR
  • High-value transactions (property, shares, etc.)
  • Incorrect deduction claims
  • Cash deposits or suspicious transactions
  • Random scrutiny selection

What Documents are Required for Notice Response?

  • Copy of Income Tax Notice
  • PAN & Aadhaar
  • Filed ITR copy
  • Form 16 / 16A
  • Bank statements
  • Investment proofs
  • Books of accounts (for business/profession)
  • Supporting documents related to the notice

What are the Penalties & Consequences of Non-Compliance?

  • Monetary penalties
  • Interest on unpaid tax
  • Additional tax demands
  • Prosecution in serious cases
  • Scrutiny assessment escalation

Due Dates for Responding to Notices

  • Usually 15 to 30 days from the date of notice
  • Varies depending on notice type
  • Delay may lead to penalties or ex-parte assessment

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What should I do after receiving an Income Tax notice?

Do not ignore it. Review the notice carefully and respond within the given timeline.

2. Can I handle the notice myself?

Yes, but professional assistance is recommended for accuracy and compliance.

3. What is scrutiny assessment?

It is a detailed examination of your ITR by the Income Tax Department.

4. What happens if I ignore the notice?

It may lead to penalties, tax demand, or legal action.

5. How long does the process take?

Depends on the complexity of the case and type of notice.
