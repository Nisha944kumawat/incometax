Tax Audit




Introduction

A Tax Audit is an examination of a taxpayer’s financial records to ensure accuracy and compliance with the provisions of the Income Tax Act, 1961. It is mandatory for certain businesses and professionals whose turnover or income exceeds prescribed limits under Section 44AB.

The purpose of a tax audit is to verify financial statements, ensure proper maintenance of books of accounts, and report discrepancies, if any. It also simplifies income tax return filing and reduces the chances of scrutiny by tax authorities.

Benefits of Tax Audit

  • Ensures Compliance with Income Tax Laws
  • Enhances Accuracy of Financial Records
  • Helps in Early Detection of Errors & Fraud
  • Reduces Risk of Tax Scrutiny
  • Improves Financial Transparency
  • Facilitates Easy Loan & Funding Approvals
  • Builds Credibility with Stakeholders

Eligibility Criteria for Tax Audit

  • Businesses with turnover exceeding ₹1 crore
  • Businesses up to ₹10 crore (if cash transactions ≤5%)
  • Professionals with receipts exceeding ₹50 lakhs
  • Assessees under presumptive taxation declaring lower income than prescribed
  • Persons required under Section 44AB of the Income Tax Act

Documents Required for Tax Audit

  • PAN & Aadhaar Card
  • Financial Statements (P&L Account and Balance Sheet)
  • Books of Accounts (Cash Book, Ledger, Sales & Purchase Register)
  • Bank Statements & Bank Reconciliation
  • GST Returns (if applicable)
  • TDS/TCS Details
  • Invoices and Expense Bills
  • Loan Statements & Investment Proofs
  • Previous Audit Reports (if any)

Tax Audit Late Fee & Penalty Details

  • Penalty under Section 271B: 0.5% of turnover or gross receipts
  • Maximum penalty: ₹1,50,000
  • Applicable if audit is not conducted or report not submitted on time
  • Penalty may be waived if reasonable cause is proven

Due Dates for Tax Audit Filing

  • Tax Audit Report (Form 3CA/3CB & 3CD): 30th September
  • Income Tax Return (Audit Cases): 31st October
  • Transfer Pricing Cases: 30th November
  • (Note: Due dates may be extended by government notifications)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. What is Section 44AB?

It specifies the conditions under which tax audit is mandatory.

Q2. Is tax audit compulsory every year?

Only if turnover/income exceeds prescribed limits or specific conditions apply.

Q3. Who can conduct a tax audit?

A Chartered Accountant in practice.

Q4. What happens if I don’t get a tax audit done?

Penalty up to ₹1,50,000 and possible scrutiny from the Income Tax Department.

Q5. Is GST audit same as tax audit?

No, GST audit and income tax audit are separate compliances.

Q6. Can audit report be revised?

Yes, in case of errors or omissions.

Q7. What is Form 3CD?

A detailed statement containing particulars required for tax audit reporting.
