GST Revocation




Introduction

GST Revocation is the process of restoring a cancelled GST registration. When a GSTIN is cancelled due to non-compliance, non-filing of returns, or other reasons, businesses can apply for revocation within the prescribed time limit. This service ensures that your registration is reinstated, allowing you to resume business operations legally and without disruption.

What are the Benefits of GST Revocation?

  • Restoration of cancelled GST registration
  • Resumption of business activities without legal restrictions
  • Avoidance of penalties and further legal complications
  • Retention of business credibility and compliance status
  • Ability to continue claiming Input Tax Credit (ITC)
  • Smooth continuation of operations

What are the Eligibility Criteria for GST Revocation?

  • Taxpayers whose GST registration has been cancelled by the department
  • Businesses willing to comply with pending requirements
  • Filing of all pending GST returns before applying
  • Application for revocation within prescribed time limits
  • Valid reasons for cancellation and request for restoration

What are the Documents Required for GST Revocation?

  • GSTIN and cancellation order
  • Pending GST return filings
  • Sales and purchase invoices
  • Proof of business activity
  • Bank statements
  • Supporting documents explaining reason for cancellation
  • Any additional documents requested by authorities

What are the GST Late Fee & Penalty Details?

  • ₹50 per day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST) for delayed return filing
  • ₹20 per day for NIL returns
  • Interest @18% per annum on unpaid tax
  • Additional penalties for prolonged non-compliance
  • Revocation may be rejected if dues are not cleared

Due Dates of Filing / Application

  • Revocation application must be filed within 30 days from the date of cancellation order
  • Extension may be granted by authorities in specific cases
  • All pending returns must be filed before applying
  • Regular GST return due dates must be followed after revocation

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is GST Revocation?

It is the process of restoring a cancelled GST registration by applying to the GST department.

2. Can I reactivate my cancelled GST registration?

Yes, by filing a revocation application within the prescribed time and clearing pending compliances.

3. What happens if I miss the revocation deadline?

You may need to apply for a new GST registration or seek extension from authorities.

4. Is filing pending returns mandatory for revocation?

Yes, all pending returns must be filed before applying.

5. How long does GST revocation take?

It depends on approval by the GST officer, usually a few working days.

6. What if my revocation application is rejected?

You may file an appeal or apply for fresh registration depending on the case.
