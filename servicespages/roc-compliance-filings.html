ROC Compliance & Filings


Introduction

ROC Compliance & Filings refer to the mandatory statutory requirements that companies and LLPs must fulfill under the Companies Act. These include annual filings, disclosures, and regular updates to the Registrar of Companies (ROC). Timely compliance ensures that your business remains legally active and avoids heavy penalties. At The Tax Titans, we provide end-to-end ROC compliance services to keep your business fully compliant and stress-free.

What are the Benefits of ROC Compliance & Filings?

  • Ensures legal compliance with corporate laws
  • Avoids heavy penalties and late fees
  • Maintains active company status
  • Builds credibility with investors and stakeholders
  • Prevents director disqualification
  • Smooth functioning of business operations

Who is Required to File ROC Compliance?

  • Private Limited Companies
  • Public Limited Companies
  • LLPs (Limited Liability Partnerships)
  • Dormant companies (limited compliance)
  • Startups registered under Companies Act

What are the Key ROC Filings?

  • AOC-4 (Financial Statements filing)
  • MGT-7 / MGT-7A (Annual Return)
  • DIR-3 KYC (Director KYC)
  • ADT-1 (Appointment of Auditor)
  • DPT-3 (Return of Deposits)
  • Event-based filings (change in director, address, etc.)

What Documents are Required?

  • PAN & Aadhaar of directors
  • CIN (Company Identification Number)
  • Financial statements
  • Board resolutions
  • Shareholding details
  • Previous ROC filings
  • Auditor details

What are the Late Fee & Penalty Details?

  • ₹100 per day for delay in ROC filings (no maximum limit in many cases)
  • Heavy additional fees for non-compliance
  • Director disqualification for continuous default
  • Company strike-off in severe cases

Due Dates of ROC Filings

  • AOC-4: Within 30 days of AGM
  • MGT-7 / MGT-7A: Within 60 days of AGM
  • DIR-3 KYC: 30th September annually
  • DPT-3: 30th June annually
  • (Due dates may change as per MCA updates)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is ROC compliance?

It refers to mandatory filings with the Registrar of Companies.

2. Is ROC filing mandatory every year?

Yes, for all registered companies and LLPs.

3. What happens if ROC filings are delayed?

Heavy penalties and possible director disqualification.

4. Can a company be closed if compliance is not done?

Yes, it may be struck off by authorities.

5. Do startups also need ROC compliance?

Yes, compliance is mandatory from incorporation.
