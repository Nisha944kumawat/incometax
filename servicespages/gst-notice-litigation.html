GST Notice & Litigation Support




Introduction

GST Notice & Litigation Support services help businesses effectively respond to notices issued by the GST department and handle legal proceedings. Notices may arise due to mismatches in returns, non-compliance, incorrect filings, or audits. Professional assistance ensures accurate replies, proper representation, and timely resolution, minimizing financial and legal risks.

What are the Benefits of GST Notice & Litigation Support?

  • Expert handling of GST notices and legal matters
  • Timely and accurate drafting of replies
  • Minimization of penalties and legal exposure
  • Proper representation before GST authorities
  • Strategic resolution of disputes
  • Peace of mind with professional compliance support

What are the Eligibility Criteria for GST Notice & Litigation Support?

  • Businesses that have received GST notices
  • Taxpayers facing GST scrutiny or audits
  • Companies involved in disputes or litigation
  • Businesses with mismatched returns or ITC issues
  • Any GST-registered entity requiring legal assistance

What are the Documents Required for GST Notice & Litigation Support?

  • Copy of GST notice received
  • GST registration certificate (GSTIN)
  • Filed GST returns (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, etc.)
  • Sales and purchase invoices
  • Supporting documents related to the case
  • Communication history with GST department
  • Financial statements (if required)

What are the GST Late Fee & Penalty Details?

  • ₹50 per day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST) for delayed return filing
  • ₹20 per day for NIL returns
  • Interest @18% per annum on delayed tax payment
  • Heavy penalties in case of fraud, misreporting, or non-compliance
  • Risk of GST registration suspension or cancellation

Due Dates of Filing / Response

  • GST notice response timelines typically range from 7 to 30 days
  • GSTR-1: 11th of the next month
  • GSTR-3B: 20th of the next month
  • Annual Return (GSTR-9): 31st December of next financial year
  • Specific deadlines depend on the type of notice issued

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is a GST notice?

A GST notice is an official communication from the GST department regarding discrepancies, non-compliance, or additional information requirements.

2. What should I do if I receive a GST notice?

Do not ignore it. Review the notice carefully and respond within the given timeline with proper documentation.

3. Can GST notices be resolved without penalties?

Yes, with timely and accurate responses, penalties can often be reduced or avoided.

4. What happens if I ignore a GST notice?

It may lead to penalties, legal action, or cancellation of GST registration.

5. Do I need professional help for GST litigation?

Yes, expert guidance ensures proper representation and increases the chances of favorable outcomes.

6. How long does GST litigation take?

It depends on the complexity of the case and the level of proceedings involved.
