Statutory Audit




Introduction

A Statutory Audit is a mandatory audit conducted to verify whether a company’s financial statements provide a true and fair view as per applicable laws such as the Companies Act, 2013 and the Income Tax Act, 1961.

It is carried out by an independent Chartered Accountant to ensure compliance, detect discrepancies, and enhance financial transparency. Statutory audits build trust among stakeholders, investors, and regulatory authorities while ensuring that businesses adhere to legal and financial reporting standards.

Benefits of Statutory Audit

  • Ensures Legal Compliance with government regulations
  • Enhances Credibility of financial statements
  • Helps in Fraud Detection and Risk Management
  • Improves Internal Controls and Efficiency
  • Builds Investor and Stakeholder Confidence
  • Assists in Better Financial Planning and Decision Making
  • Required for loans, funding, and tenders

Eligibility Criteria for Audit (Tax Audit Applicability)

  • Businesses with turnover exceeding ₹1 crore (₹10 crore in specified digital cases)
  • Professionals with gross receipts exceeding ₹50 lakhs
  • Businesses declaring income under presumptive taxation but income lower than prescribed limits
  • Assessees required under specific sections of Income Tax Act (e.g., Section 44AB)
  • Companies and LLPs (audit mandatory irrespective of turnover under Companies Act)

Documents Required for Audit

  • PAN Card and Aadhaar of assessee
  • Financial Statements (Profit & Loss A/c, Balance Sheet)
  • Books of Accounts (Ledger, Cash Book, Purchase/Sales Register)
  • Bank Statements and Reconciliation
  • GST Returns and Records
  • TDS Returns and Details
  • Previous Year Audit Reports (if applicable)
  • Invoices and Expense Bills
  • Loan Agreements and Investment Proofs

Audit Late Fee & Penalty Details

  • Penalty under Section 271B: 0.5% of turnover or gross receipts
  • Maximum penalty: ₹1,50,000
  • Penalty applicable for failure to get accounts audited or submit audit report on time
  • Penalty may be waived if reasonable cause is proven (as per Income Tax provisions)

Due Dates of Filing (Audit Cases)

  • Tax Audit Report (Form 3CA/3CB & 3CD): 30th September (generally extended in some years)
  • Income Tax Return (Audit Cases): 31st October
  • Transfer Pricing Cases: 30th November
  • (Note: Dates may vary as per government notifications)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. Is statutory audit mandatory for all businesses?

No, it depends on turnover and type of entity. However, companies and LLPs must get audited mandatorily.

Q2. What is the difference between statutory audit and tax audit?

Statutory audit is under the Companies Act, while tax audit is under the Income Tax Act.

Q3. Who can conduct a statutory audit?

Only a qualified Chartered Accountant in practice.

Q4. What happens if audit is not conducted?

Penalty under Income Tax Act and possible legal consequences.

Q5. Can audit due dates be extended?

Yes, the government may extend due dates via notifications.

Q6. Is audit required for freelancers?

Only if income exceeds prescribed limits or specific conditions apply.

Q7. What is Form 3CD?

It is a detailed statement of particulars required for tax audit filing.
