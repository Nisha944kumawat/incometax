NGO & Trust Registrations In India


Introduction

NGO & Trust Registration is the legal process of forming a non-profit organization for charitable, religious, educational, or social purposes. Common structures in India include Trusts, Societies, and Section 8 Companies. Registration provides legal identity and enables organizations to operate transparently while accessing government and donor funding.

Benefits of Registration

  • Legal recognition as a non-profit organization
  • Eligibility for tax exemptions such as 12A & 80G registration
  • Ability to receive donations and grants legally
  • Access to government funding and CSR contributions
  • Enhanced credibility and donor trust
  • Separate legal identity and structured governance
  • Eligibility for foreign funding through FCRA registration
  • Long-term sustainability and transparency

Eligibility Criteria for Registration

  • Minimum 2 trustees for Trust Registration
  • Defined charitable or religious objective for Trust Registration
  • Trust Deed required for Trust Registration
  • Minimum 7 members for Society Registration
  • Purpose must be for social welfare, education, or charity
  • Minimum 2 directors for Private Section 8 Company
  • Minimum 3 directors for Public Section 8 Company
  • Non-profit objective with no profit distribution
  • Income must be used for promoting organizational objectives

Documents Required for Registration

  • PAN Card of members/trustees/directors
  • Aadhaar Card / ID proof
  • Passport-size photographs
  • Address proof of registered office
  • Trust Deed for Trust Registration
  • NOC from property owner
  • Memorandum of Association (MOA) for Society Registration
  • Rules & Regulations for Society Registration
  • MOA & AOA for Section 8 Company
  • Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)
  • Director Identification Number (DIN)

Late Fee & Penalty Details

  • Delay in registration may restrict legal operations
  • Penalties for non-compliance under Income Tax Act
  • Late filing of returns may attract fines and penalties
  • Section 8 Companies may face ROC penalties for non-compliance
  • FCRA violations may lead to heavy penalties or cancellation
  • Misuse of funds may result in legal action

Due Dates of Filing

  • Income Tax Return: 31st July / 31st October, as applicable
  • Annual Filing for Section 8 Company: As per ROC deadlines
  • 12A / 80G Compliance: As per Income Tax provisions
  • FCRA Return: 31st December annually
  • Society/Trust Compliance: As per state laws

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. Which is better: Trust, Society, or Section 8 Company?

It depends on scale, governance needs, and funding requirements.

Q2. Is 80G registration mandatory?

No, but it helps donors claim tax deductions, increasing donations.

Q3. Can NGOs earn profit?

They can generate income, but it must be used for charitable purposes only.

Q4. How long does registration take?

Typically 7–15 working days depending on the structure.

Q5. Can NGOs receive foreign donations?

Yes, but FCRA registration is mandatory.

Q6. Is annual compliance compulsory?

Yes, to maintain legal status and tax benefits.
