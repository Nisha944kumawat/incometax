Internal Audit




Introduction

Internal Audit is an independent and systematic evaluation of an organization’s internal controls, risk management processes, and operational efficiency. Unlike statutory or tax audits, internal audits are conducted to improve business processes, strengthen controls, and ensure smooth functioning of operations.

It helps management identify weaknesses, prevent fraud, and enhance overall governance. Internal audits can be conducted periodically based on the size, nature, and risk profile of the business.

Benefits of Internal Audit

  • Strengthens Internal Controls and Processes
  • Identifies Risks and Prevents Fraud
  • Improves Operational Efficiency
  • Ensures Compliance with Policies & Regulations
  • Enhances Financial Accuracy and Reporting
  • Supports Better Decision Making
  • Builds Strong Corporate Governance

Eligibility / Applicability of Internal Audit

  • Companies as per Companies Act, 2013 (based on turnover, paid-up capital, or outstanding loans)
  • Large Private Companies meeting prescribed thresholds
  • Listed Companies (mandatory)
  • Certain Unlisted Public Companies
  • Businesses seeking better control, even if not mandatory (voluntary internal audit)

Documents Required for Internal Audit

  • Financial Statements (P&L, Balance Sheet)
  • Books of Accounts and Ledgers
  • Internal Policies & SOPs
  • Bank Statements and Reconciliations
  • GST, TDS and Compliance Records
  • Invoices, Bills, and Supporting Documents
  • Payroll and Employee Records
  • Previous Audit Reports (if available)

Internal Audit – Penalty & Compliance Aspect

  • No direct penalty for not conducting internal audit (if not applicable)
  • For companies where internal audit is mandatory, non-compliance may lead to:
  • Penalties under the Companies Act
  • Increased regulatory scrutiny
  • Weak internal controls leading to financial risks
  • Indirect losses due to fraud, errors, or inefficiencies if audit is not conducted

Due Dates / Frequency of Internal Audit

  • No fixed statutory due date
  • Conducted:
  • Quarterly / Half-Yearly / Annually (based on company policy)
  • For companies under Companies Act: As prescribed by Board / Audit Committee

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. Is internal audit mandatory for all businesses?

No, only for certain companies as per Companies Act. Others can opt voluntarily.

Q2. Who conducts internal audit?

It can be conducted by a Chartered Accountant, Internal Auditor, or independent professional.

Q3. What is the difference between internal and statutory audit?

Internal audit focuses on process improvement; statutory audit focuses on legal compliance.

Q4. How often should internal audit be conducted?

Depends on business size and risk—commonly quarterly or annually.

Q5. Can a small business do internal audit?

Yes, it helps improve efficiency and control even if not mandatory.

Q6. Does internal audit detect fraud?

Yes, it helps in identifying and preventing fraud risks.

Q7. Is internal audit useful for startups?

Yes, it helps set strong systems and avoid future financial issues.
