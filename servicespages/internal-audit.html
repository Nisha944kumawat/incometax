What is Internal Audit?

Internal Audit is an independent evaluation of an organization's processes, systems, controls, and operational activities. The objective is to assess business risks, identify control weaknesses, improve efficiency, and ensure effective management of resources.

A properly conducted internal audit supports informed decision-making, strengthens internal controls, and promotes sustainable business growth.

Key Features of Internal Audit:

  • Comprehensive process and system evaluation
  • Risk assessment and internal control review

  • Identification of operational improvement opportunities

  • Fraud detection and prevention support
  • Enhanced efficiency, governance, and accountability
